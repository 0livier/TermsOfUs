# Relationship anarchy framing copy

This note proposes a light copy adjustment for Terms of Us after reviewing public explanations of relationship anarchy.

The goal is not to make the app a manifesto or to require users to identify with relationship anarchy. The goal is to make the framing more precise: Terms of Us helps people clarify what a relationship contains, could contain, or should not contain for them, while also noticing which expectations may come from inherited relationship scripts.

## Suggested framing

Prefer:

> a tool to clarify what a relationship contains, could contain, or should not contain

Over:

> a tool to express expectations and boundaries

The second wording is still useful, but it can sound more contractual. The first one is closer to the Smorgasbord metaphor and to relationship anarchy's focus on designing each relationship directly rather than inheriting a default template.

## Suggested English intro body

```text
Terms of Us helps you put words on a relationship: what is already there, what you would like, what deserves a conversation, and what does not work for you.

This is not a score or a test. It is a way to notice what might deserve words.

It can also help you separate what you genuinely want from what familiar relationship scripts say a relationship is supposed to contain.

Start with the categories that speak to you. You do not need to fill in everything: the goal is to identify what truly matters to you.

You can then review your summary, read it back, and share it if you want to open a conversation.

It is inspired by the Relationship Anarchy Smorgasbord, a discussion tool created in relationship anarchy communities to help clarify what each relationship can contain.
```

## Suggested Learn more section

Add this section after the current “What this tool helps with” / “Ce que l’outil permet” section.

### English

Title:

```text
Questioning relationship scripts
```

Body:

```text
Relationship anarchy invites people to question implicit hierarchies between relationships: for example, the idea that a romantic couple should always come before a friendship, that an important relationship should follow a predetermined progression, or that there is only one right way to love, commit, or care for each other.

Terms of Us does not ask you to adopt a label or a relationship model. It simply offers a space to look at each relationship more directly: what is alive here, what is missing, what does not fit, and what deserves a conversation?
```

### French

```text
Questionner les scripts relationnels
```

Body:

```text
L’anarchie relationnelle invite à questionner les hiérarchies implicites entre les relations : par exemple l’idée qu’un couple devrait toujours passer avant une amitié, qu’une relation importante devrait suivre une progression attendue, ou qu’il existerait une seule bonne manière d’aimer, de s’engager ou de prendre soin les un·es des autres.

Terms of Us ne vous demande pas d’adopter une étiquette ou un modèle relationnel. Il propose simplement un espace pour regarder chaque relation plus directement : qu’est-ce qui est vivant ici, qu’est-ce qui manque, qu’est-ce qui ne convient pas, qu’est-ce qui mérite une conversation ?
```

## Guardrails

- Keep the home page soft and practical.
- Avoid turning Terms of Us into a manifesto.
- Keep the stronger political and philosophical framing in “Learn more”.
- Keep repeating that the tool is not a score, a compatibility test, or a diagnostic tool.
- Keep privacy wording clear: local by default, but shared links contain answers.
